Activity 1: Dataset Understanding SummaryThe Iris Flower Dataset (also known as Fisher's Iris Dataset) is a foundational, multivariate dataset introduced by the British statistician and biologist Ronald Fisher in 1936. It consists of 150 instances evenly divided among three species of Iris flowers: Iris setosa, Iris versicolor, and Iris virginica (50 samples each).  Four continuous geometric features—sepal length, sepal width, petal length, and petal width—were measured in centimeters for each flower. A striking and notable structural property of this dataset is that Iris setosa is linearly separable from the other two species, whereas Iris versicolor and Iris virginica exhibit slight overlap and are not completely linearly separable from each other. Because of its balance, lack of missing values, and simple structure, it serves as the ultimate "Hello World" benchmark for testing statistical classification and clustering algorithms.
